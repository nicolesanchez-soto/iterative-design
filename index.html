Allara Health: Messaging Inbox Redesign

As Allara is a virtual healthcare platform, their main point of contact with users is via their messaging system. The company expressed wishes to redesign their messaging system to better streamline user experience. Concerns that have been raised by patients included difficulty finding past messages due to paor type hierarchy and unclear subject lisnes.

The way the current messaging process is set-up has users speaking with a concierge team as opposed to an assigned team member, meaning conversations can continue across members or several threads could be open with a certain concierge. The current lack of filtering and organization in the message inbox makes it difficult for users to search for specific messages which has caused users to express frustration.

Specific restraints we were given were that users cannot create message threads directly with providers, nor are they able to close threads.

Sketching and Wireframing

We began addressing the issue by doing some research on other messaging platforms we have used on desktop and mobile devices. We reimagined a design with our ideas using rough sketches for how we could compose a new design that addressed some user pain points with the aim to condense them into a single wireframe.

Common among all of them were the desire for a search bar, filter buttons, and subject line emphasis. Other ideas we had included ways to organize closed messages, making message previews simpler, and adding an additional tab with easy access to relevant thread information.

Critique & Hi-Fi Prototype

The wireframe model was created using Balsamiq and combined some of the key elements we imagined would improve on the user pain points. These include restructuring type hierarchies, Topic and Concierge Member filters, a thread details tab, and an archive feature. With these new features, we were hoping to improve the organization in a way that would allow users to quickly find important information. This wireframe sets the foundation for further prototyping and feedback from our stakeholders.

Final Feedback

During our last meeting, Liv shared some valuable insights on our Hi-Fi prototype. A few key takeaways included:

Color palette: Liv highlighted the importance of avoiding colors that might reinforce stereotypes like purples and pinks especially on a platform designed for women. As a result, we would explore more inclusive and professional tones like black or blue to better align with our values.

Messaging interface: She also observed that our messaging layout closely resembles an email interface. Moving forward, we'll either lean into this with stronger reasoning for that design choice or refine the interface to feel more conversational and less "inbox-like."

Feature prioritization: Features such as starring and archiving were flagged as potentially unnecessary at this stage. Our next nest would be streamlining by removing these and doubling down on core functionalities that matter most to users such as search and organization.

Next steps: As a team, we also agreed to focus on improving the overall messaging experience with Allara Concierge.

Key Takeaway: Every Design detail no matter how small plays a role in shaping the user experience. Throughout the course, this takeaway becomes more apparent and Liv's insights served as an important reminder.
