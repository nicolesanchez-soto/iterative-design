Allara Health: Messaging Inbox Redesign

Allara Health is a virtual care platform designed to support women—primarily between the ages of 20 and 40—managing hormonal and metabolic conditions like PCOS. Since the platform centers on digital communication, its messaging system plays a crucial role in how patients connect with their care team. But based on the feedback we received, the current setup is falling short.

Users described the inbox as confusing and difficult to navigate. Messages are grouped by topic rather than person, and because patients speak with a rotating concierge team instead of a dedicated provider, multiple threads can stack up with similar subject lines but no clear continuity. Add in the lack of filtering and search, and it becomes especially frustrating for people trying to reference important information—like lab results or medication instructions—weeks or months after a conversation took place.

When you’re already carrying the emotional weight of managing a chronic condition, the interface shouldn't add to your stress. That insight guided everything we did in this redesign: make it easier to find, follow, and manage conversations with care.

Sketching and Wireframing

We began by exploring familiar platforms—texting apps, customer support portals, inboxes—and then created four sets of hand-drawn sketches to brainstorm alternatives. Each sketch proposed a different layout, but across the board, we prioritized:

  • Search bars for finding messages quickly
  • Filters by concierge and topic
  • Simplified previews with emphasized subject lines
  • A tab for viewing thread details, attachments, and context

After reviewing and combining the strongest elements from each design, we created a low-fidelity wireframe in Balsamiq. This version consolidated our ideas into a single, cohesive flow with a clean layout and added clarity around message groupings. One notable change was experimenting with a top navigation bar on desktop for a more modern feel.

In critique, we received two clear pieces of feedback: the desktop version should maintain a traditional left sidebar navigation, and we should only filter by Concierge, not Provider, to reflect how communication actually works at Allara. These were important reminders to design with technical and organizational realities in mind.

Critique & Hi-Fi Prototype

Moving into high-fidelity, we focused on translating structure into a polished, interactive experience using Figma. The visual language became more refined, but the core ideas from our wireframe stayed central. Our design aimed to feel both professional and calming—supportive, not sterile.

Key features included:

  • Search and filter tools for reducing information overload
  • The ability to star and archive messages for easier long-term access
  • Thread views grouped by concierge to help users follow conversations with specific team members
  • Clean, scannable message cards with bolded subject lines, timestamps, and clear read/unread states

These features weren’t added for flash—they directly answered real user frustrations. We designed this prototype not just to look real, but to behave like something someone could use tomorrow.

Final Feedback

During our final critique session, we presented our Hi-Fi prototype and received thoughtful feedback from Liv that helped us reflect on both our design decisions and future possibilities.

Color palette: Liv highlighted the importance of avoiding colors that might reinforce stereotypes, such as purples and pinks—especially on a platform designed for women. As a result, we would explore more inclusive and professional tones like black or blue to better align with our values.

Messaging interface: She also noted that our layout strongly resembled an email interface. Moving forward, we would consider either leaning into this format with clearer intent or refining the flow to feel more conversational and approachable.

Feature prioritization: Features such as starring and archiving were flagged as potentially unnecessary for this stage. Liv encouraged us to streamline by focusing on core functionalities that matter most to users—like search and filtering.

Next steps: Had we had more time, we would have incorporated this feedback to further refine visual tone, layout style, and feature prioritization. As a team, we still see opportunities to improve the overall messaging experience with Allara Concierge.

Key Takeaway: Every design detail—no matter how small—shapes how users feel. Liv's insights reminded us that good design means making intentional choices that respect users’ needs, emotions, and contexts.
